Declarations of Independence


Texas Declaration of Independence


This is from when the Mexican state of Texas-Coahuila had to deal with impossible problems with Texas's previously promised right to statehood when the Texas area of the state of Texas-Coahuila had sufficient population and infrastructure. The government of México had essentially failed and been replaced. Thus that promised statehood could not be obtained from the acting Government of México. At that time, Texas was still a part of México.



The Thirteen Colonies at War with Great Britain


The Declaration explained why the Thirteen Colonies at war with the Kingdom of Great Britain regarded themselves as thirteen independent sovereign states, no longer under British rule.
Please note that at this time, there did not yet exist a nation called "The United States of America".
Many years would pass before the Constitution was written.
After the war, but before the Constitution, The Articles of Confederation would form a loose grouping of the states also called "The United States of America". Despite the same names, they should be considered two different entities.

Constitutions


Articles of Confederation of The United States


The Second Continental Congress produced The Articles of Confederation on November 15, 1777
This is the first Confederation which used the name "The United States of America".



Constitution of the United States of America


This is from after the intended revision of the Articles of Confederation.
The Constitution of the United States of America was technically written illegally.
Nevertheless, it was ratified by the number of States required.



Constitution of the Confederate States of America


It was adopted on March 11, 1861 to form the Confederate States of America.
It was in effect from February 22, 1862 to the conclusion of the American Civil War.

Historical Laws


Hammurabi's Legal Code


Hammurabi's Legal Code was written around 1780BC. It is the earliest known set code of justice.
In other places, justice was not a fixed code, but rather a system of justice set down at the moment by whoever decided each case.

Historical Economies


Different Economical Systems


A vast number of economic systems have been tried throughout history.
Frequently, even the same political entities have tried a large variety for themselves.
For example, it is not unreasonable to consider the Roman Kingdom, the Roman Republic and the Roman Empire to essentially be composed of the same people, but trying many different systems during that long historical period.
This section will try to enumerate some of these systems and their successes and failures.
